I'm Eric Harrison.

I'm a software engineer, writer, and entrepreneur focused on building awesome and innovative computer-based learning products. I'm crazy passionate about improving education and think that creative software solutions will empower our future generations.

About Me

I am a professional engineer with over 15 years of experience designing, developing, and launching innovative data-centric applications. Over the last 2 years, I've been working passionately at Moonrise trying to make the world a better place by providing hard-workers with a steady source of reliable jobs.

I'm also passionate about the business of running software companies. I like to improve painful and inefficient processes whenever I can and love to write winning proposals to generate new business.

Work

Moonrise, Inc

Co-Founder and CTO • January 2017 - Present

At Moonrise, we're trying to make the world a better place by providing hard-working Americans with a steady source of shifts at reputable employers. Our engineering team built a fully-automated real-time staffing solution that matches the perfect worker with amazing jobs using a combination of data science and an interactive chatbot through text messaging.

ISC Consulting Group

VP of Engineering • May 2014 - January 2017

At ISC, my team took a "wholistic" approach to training and first spent a significant amount of time engaged with the customer and learning where they had training gaps that could be improved by a mix of computer-based learning, games, and simulations. Over a span of two years, we developed over 200 hours of interactive courseware, 2 flash games, and one immersive VR simulation.

The Angelo Group

Lead Engineer • August 2012 - May 2014

The Angelo Group was my first experience working at a training company. We created several awesome products. My favorite was a cool "Choose Your Own Adventure" interactive movie that allowed learners to control the plot of a series of training vignettes. My team also developed a NodeJS-based Interactive Multimedia Instruction (IMI) development product that allowed training developers to rapidly create interactive courseware.
